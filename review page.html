The Brisket: This is the litmus test for any smokehouse, and Honeywood passes with flying colors. It features a deep, peppery bark and a stunning smoke ring. The fat is rendered down to a buttery consistency, making every bite incredibly succulent. The Ribs & Poultry: The St. Louis-style ribs have the perfect "bite"—tender enough to pull away clean but with enough structure to satisfy. Even the smoked chicken, often an afterthought elsewhere, is remarkably juicy and infused with a delicate hickory sweetness. Elevated Sides: Most places treat sides as fillers, but here, the Bourbon Pit Beans and Jalapeño Cornbread are mandatory orders. The mac & cheese is velvety and topped with a toasted crumb that adds a perfect textural contrast. Hospitality: The service is fast and friendly without feeling rushed. The staff clearly takes pride in the "low and slow" process, and their passion for the craft shows in their recommendations.

The presentation of the "Pitmaster's Bounty" is an immediate visual triumph, featuring a massive, high-walled metal tray lined with classic red-and-white checkered paper that perfectly captures the authentic smokehouse tradition. The ribs serve as the clear stars of the show, boasting a gorgeous mahogany glaze and a clean "pull-off-the-bone" tenderness that reveals a deep-pink smoke ring beneath the surface. Not to be outdone, the half-chicken is a masterclass in texture, with skin rendered to a crisp, golden brown while the meat remains remarkably moist. Tucked into the corners are the staples of the pit; the brisket is sliced thick to highlight its buttery fat cap and peppery bark, while the pulled pork is piled high with a flavorful mix of tender inner meat and coveted crispy bark bits. This rich, fatty spread is balanced by the sharp acidity of thick-cut pickles and a variety of house-made sauces, ranging from sweet honey-base to tangy vinegar mops, which allow for a fully customized experience. Ultimately, this generous tray is a soulful masterclass in barbecue designed for sharing, proving that Honeywood doesn't just cook food, but truly honors the "low and slow" tradition of the craft.
